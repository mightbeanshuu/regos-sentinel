# Validation and Claim Discipline

## Current evidence status

The repository distinguishes implemented visualization, planned prototype behavior, target metrics, and measured results.

| Evidence type | Current status |

| Measured extraction metrics | Not yet reported |

| Sandbox evaluation | Proposed |

## Metric contract

Future evaluation should report:

- obligation precision and recall;
- citation correctness;
- applicability accuracy;
- low-confidence exception capture;
- material human-edit rate;
- source-to-review latency;
- evidence freshness-test accuracy;
- safe-failure behavior.

Targets must be labeled as targets until reproduced by a documented evaluation.

## Demo truth rules

- Show `Prototype visualization` throughout the motion film.
- Label synthetic entities and evidence.
- Label simulated connectors.
- Do not present animation as proof that a backend exists.
- Do not claim `100% accuracy`, guaranteed compliance, or autonomous legal judgment.
- Do not use customer, pilot, or endorsement language without permission and evidence.

## Pre-submission checks

1. Every legal or regulatory statement has a source.
2. Every product claim maps to implemented behavior or is labeled roadmap.
3. Every displayed metric is measured or labeled target.
4. The demo contains one controlled failure and human correction.
5. The final ask names a bounded next test.
